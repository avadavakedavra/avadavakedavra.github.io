CBSE Class 12 · Board Exam Guide 2025–26

CBSE Class 12 Chapter-Wise
Weightage 2025–26

A complete, subject-wise breakdown of marks distribution for the CBSE Class 12 board examinations. Know which chapters carry the highest weightage in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics and English — and where to focus your preparation for maximum marks.

Physics Chemistry Biology Mathematics English
By SEHR Academy · Vadanappally, Thrissur · Updated June 2026

Class 12 board marks follow you — for college admissions, for NEET and JEE cutoffs, for scholarships, and for everything that comes after school. The students who score 90+ are not those who studied every line of every chapter equally. They are the ones who knew exactly where the marks are and put their preparation hours there. This guide gives you the official unit-wise and estimated chapter-wise marks distribution for all five core Science stream subjects in the 2025–26 academic year, based on CBSE's official syllabus documents, sample papers, and question paper trends from the past five years.

Important note: CBSE publishes official unit-wise weightage but does not release a formal chapter-wise breakdown. The chapter-level distribution in this guide is derived from the official CBSE syllabus (2025–26), CBSE sample papers, and five-year question paper analysis. Unit-wise marks are authoritative; chapter-wise marks are well-informed estimates for strategic preparation.

How CBSE Class 12 Board Exams Work

Each CBSE Class 12 Science subject has a total of 100 marks. For Science subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), this is split 70 marks theory + 30 marks practical. For Mathematics and English Core, it is 80 marks theory + 20 marks internal assessment — identical to the Class 10 structure.

The theory paper follows a prescribed format set out in each subject's CBSE marking scheme. Questions include MCQs and assertion-reason (1 mark), very short answer (2 marks), short answer (3 marks), long answer (5 marks), and case/source-based questions (4–5 marks). Competency-based questions now account for roughly 40% of the paper across subjects.

Practicals matter at 12: Unlike Class 10 where practical marks are split across periodic tests, at Class 12 the 30-mark practical exam is held at the board level — an external examiner assesses your experiments, record book, and viva. Many students lose 6–8 practical marks through careless record writing. Treat the practical as seriously as the theory.

Physics — Chapter-Wise Weightage

Physics (Code 042) is one of the most marks-intensive papers. Electrostatics is the single largest unit at 15 marks, followed by Optics (14 marks), Current Electricity (7 marks) and Magnetic Effects + Magnetism (8 marks). Together these four unit groups account for 44 out of 70 marks — nearly two-thirds of the paper. The Class 12 Physics paper is known for its application-based numerical problems, and practicals add another 30 marks.

Physics Strategy: Electrostatics (15 marks) and Optics (14 marks) together are nearly 42% of the paper. Numericals from Coulomb's law, Gauss's theorem, Young's experiment, and lens/mirror equations appear in almost every paper. Master these two units completely before moving to smaller units like EM Waves (3 marks) or Dual Nature (4 marks).

Chemistry — Chapter-Wise Weightage

Chemistry (Code 043) is divided into Physical Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry and Organic Chemistry. Organic Chemistry chapters dominate the paper — Aldehydes, Ketones and Carboxylic Acids (8 marks), Electrochemistry (9 marks) and Coordination Compounds (7 marks) are the three highest-scoring chapters. The paper rewards students who understand reaction mechanisms and name reactions, not those who only memorise formulas.

Chemistry Strategy: Electrochemistry (9 marks) is the single highest-scoring chapter — learn Nernst equation, cell reactions, and conductance calculations thoroughly. In Organic Chemistry, the name reactions across Chapters 6–9 (Haloalkanes, Alcohols, Aldehydes, Amines) carry significant weight — make a consolidated list of all 20+ named reactions and practise writing them from memory.

Biology — Chapter-Wise Weightage

Biology (Code 044) is often the highest-scoring subject among Science stream students. Genetics and Evolution is the biggest unit at 18 marks, followed by Reproduction (14 marks), Biology in Human Welfare (14 marks) and Ecology (14 marks). The paper is heavily diagram-based — well-labelled diagrams can earn you 2–3 extra marks per question and are indispensable for the higher mark questions.

Biology Strategy: Genetics and Evolution (18 marks) is the highest-scoring unit — Principles of Inheritance and Molecular Basis of Inheritance alone account for 15 marks. NEET aspirants should give this unit even more time as it is also one of the most heavily tested sections in the medical entrance exam. Practise Mendelian cross problems and DNA replication diagrams daily.

Mathematics — Chapter-Wise Weightage

Mathematics (Code 041) is the highest-stakes paper for JEE aspirants. Calculus is by far the dominant unit, carrying 35 marks — nearly 44% of the entire paper. Vectors and 3D Geometry (14 marks) and Algebra — Matrices and Determinants (10 marks) are the next two high-priority areas. The paper rewards speed and accuracy, particularly in Calculus and 3D problems.

Mathematics Strategy: Calculus is 35 marks out of 80 — a student who fully masters Chapters 5–9 can secure nearly half the paper. Applications of Derivatives (10 marks) is the highest-value single chapter in all of Class 12 Maths. Combined with 3D Geometry (8 marks), just these two chapters cover 22.5% of the total paper. Solve minimum 30 problems from each before the boards.

English Core — Paper Structure and Weightage

English Core (Code 301) at Class 12 follows a skills-based structure rather than chapter weightage. The paper tests Reading, Writing, Grammar and Literature. Literature is the highest-scoring section at 40 marks — significantly higher than at Class 10. The texts are from Flamingo (prose and poems) and Vistas (supplementary reader).

English note: The internal assessment 20 marks are split across: listening and speaking activities (10 marks), reading project (5 marks), and portfolio (5 marks). These are entirely school-controlled and predictable — students who stay diligent with these tasks should comfortably secure 16–18 marks without extra effort.

For NEET and JEE Aspirants

If you are targeting NEET or JEE alongside the boards, the weightage overlap is significant — but not identical. NEET heavily emphasises Genetics, Molecular Biology, Human Physiology, and Ecology. JEE adds topics not in CBSE boards (like complex numbers, coordinate geometry) but Class 12 CBSE Calculus and 3D Geometry form the backbone of JEE Maths.

Smart overlap strategy: Do not treat CBSE boards and competitive exams as two separate preparations. Mastering CBSE board content at depth — particularly Electrostatics, Optics, Genetics, and Calculus — directly serves NEET and JEE preparation. Board exam practice also forces you to write out full solutions, which competitive-exam MCQ practice alone does not.

How to Use Weightage for Smart Preparation

  • 1Front-load your high-weightage units. In Physics, begin with Electrostatics and Optics. In Biology, open with Genetics and Evolution. In Maths, go straight to Calculus. These are the units that test the broadest range of concepts and require the most repetition.
  • 2Do not underestimate short units. EM Waves (3 marks in Physics) and Linear Programming (5 marks in Maths) are short, focused chapters. A student who spends three hours on each can comfortably score full marks on those questions — don't leave them for the week before the exam.
  • 3Take your practicals seriously. At 30 marks each for Physics, Chemistry and Biology, practicals are not a bonus — they are a core component. Keep your record book updated, practise titration and experiment procedures, and do not underestimate the viva component where many students drop marks.
  • 4Use CBSE sample papers and past question papers. The official CBSE sample paper and marking scheme released each year are the most accurate preview of the actual exam. Solve at least three years of papers for each subject in timed conditions — this builds both content confidence and time management.
  • 5Master name reactions in Chemistry. CBSE Class 12 Chemistry papers consistently carry 6–8 marks from named reactions (Aldol condensation, Cannizzaro, Sandmeyer, Reimer-Tiemann, etc.). Make a one-page summary of all name reactions with reactants, conditions, and products, and revise it every two weeks.
  • 6Draw diagrams to think, not just to answer. Biology and Physics diagrams carry explicit marks. But beyond that, practising a diagram of the replication fork, the electromagnetic spectrum, or an LCR circuit actually builds conceptual clarity. Use diagrams as a primary learning tool, not an afterthought.
