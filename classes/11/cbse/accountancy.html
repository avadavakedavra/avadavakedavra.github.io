How to Score Well

Exam Strategy

What we tell every Class 11 student at the start of the year — the habits and priorities that consistently lead to high scores.

01

Make Unit 2 your priority from Day 1

Journal entries, Ledger, BRS, Depreciation and Final Accounts together carry 60+ marks. These are all skill-based — you get better with practice, not reading. Solve at least 5 numericals per chapter before moving on.

02

Don't skip Theory Base of Accounting

Chapter 2 (GAAP concepts) feels dry, but boards regularly set 2–4 mark questions on it. "Distinguish between Going Concern and Consistency" is exactly the kind of question that catches students who only practiced numericals.

03

Learn BRS both ways — from CB and from PB

The Bank Reconciliation Statement question can start from either the Cash Book balance or the Pass Book balance. Practice both. The format changes, the logic doesn't. This is a reliable 6-mark question every year.

04

Adjustments in Final Accounts — one by one

Each adjustment affects two places in the Final Accounts. Master them one at a time, then combine. Students who try to learn all adjustments at once always get confused. Work through them in the NCERT order.

05

Solve 3 years of board papers

CBSE Accountancy questions follow recognisable patterns. After Chapter 10, shift to solving past years — you'll see the same numerical types appear repeatedly. This is the fastest way to predict what will come this year.

06

Secure the internal 20 marks

Internal assessment is completely in your control. Submit the project on time, maintain the practical file, and prepare for the Viva. Twenty free marks is the difference between 70% and 90% — don't take it lightly.

Our Approach

Accountancy requires a specific kind of teaching — one that builds concept clarity first, then drills the numericals, then tests under exam conditions. Here is how we do that.

🧱

Concept Before Calculation

We spend time making sure students understand why each entry is made before drilling the format. Students who understand the logic make fewer errors and recover faster when they forget a step.

🔁

Daily Numerical Practice

Journal entries, BRS problems and Depreciation questions are practiced every session until the pattern is automatic. Accountancy is a skill, not a subject to memorise.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Answers to what students and parents most commonly ask before joining Class 11 Accountancy at SEHR Academy.

01 I'm new to commerce — will I be able to follow accountancy from scratch?

Yes, absolutely. Class 11 Accountancy starts from zero — there is no prior accounting knowledge assumed. Chapter 1 begins with the basic question "what is accounting and why does it exist?" We teach every student as if it's their first time, because for Class 11 it always is.

02 Which is harder — CBSE or Kerala State Board accountancy?

The core concepts are very similar. CBSE follows NCERT textbooks and tends to be more standardised in question patterns, which makes exam preparation more systematic. Kerala State Board (SCERT) is activity-based and has a different exam style. CBSE Accountancy is well-suited for students planning CA Foundation, as ICAI content closely mirrors CBSE.

03 Is Class 11 Accountancy important for CA Foundation?

Extremely important. CA Foundation Paper 1 (Fundamentals of Accounting) tests content that directly overlaps with Class 11 and 12 Accountancy — Journal entries, Ledger, Final Accounts, Depreciation, BRS. Students who master Class 11 have a significant head start on CA Foundation and often find the paper easier than their non-commerce peers.

04 Which chapters are most important for the CBSE board exam?

In order of marks weightage:

  • Chapter 10 (Financial Statements II with Adjustments) — the most complex and highest-value question
  • Chapter 7 (Depreciation) — SLM and WDV numericals are guaranteed
  • Chapter 5 (BRS) — a standalone 6-mark numerical appears every year
  • Chapter 6 (Rectification of Errors) — rectification journal entries are always present
  • Chapter 12 (NPO Accounts) — Part B's highest-weightage chapter

But do not neglect Chapters 1–2. Short-answer questions from the theoretical framework are easy marks that students skip at their peril.

05 Do you cover the Tally / computerised accounting chapters?

Yes. We cover Unit 5 (Computers in Accounting) both for the theory questions in the board exam and practically for project work. We walk through basic Tally operations — company creation, ledger grouping and voucher entries — so students can handle their practical file and project confidently.

06 What textbooks do you follow at SEHR Academy?

Primary: NCERT Accountancy Parts I and II (official CBSE textbooks). Supplementary: DK Goel (for extra numericals) and T.S. Grewal (for additional practice and board-pattern questions). We do not burden students with multiple books — NCERT is sufficient for the board, supplementary books are used selectively for specific chapters.

07 How many hours per week is the accountancy coaching?

Class 11 Accountancy is typically 3 sessions per week (each 90 minutes), totalling approximately 4–5 hours of classroom time weekly. During exam season and before boards, we add revision and test sessions. Homework and self-practice of numericals are expected between sessions — these are as important as the classroom time.
